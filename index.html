    The Project

    The purpose of this project is to portray in an easily digestible manner the biogeographic research of Rachel Isaacs, a Penn State Ph.D. candidate, in Denali National Park and Preserve. Isaacs’ study is presented on an interactive website to a general audience of all ages and educational backgrounds. The interface portrays the research process as a story, following a logical progression through the identification of climate change in Alaska, how fieldwork is performed, and the results of scientific investigation. Interactive Alaska climate change maps allow the user to compare spatial temperature patterns over time. A description of field methods explains technical concepts including GIS, remote sensing, and land cover change. An interactive Denali map enables site-specific exploration of research results. Following the guidelines of Nielson’s (1995) usability heuristics we empower the user to navigate the application with complete control.
